About Me

Hello! I'm Diego Patterson, a Computer Science student based in Riverside, California. I specialize in Bash Scripting and am a junior.

My journey into technology began when I was around 12. I wasn’t the sharpest tool in the shed and fell for a free Robux scam. Later, as a freshman in high school, my sister returned from a camp at Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology and taught me a bit of Python. However, it never really took off for me until COVID.

Due to the quarantine, my teacher needed a less resource-intensive way to teach game design, so he settled on JavaScript. This is where I discovered my passion.

In college, as my interest in computer science deepened, it became part of my everyday life. I found a game called Hacknet, where you play as a gray-hat hacker in a group reminiscent of Anonymous. Though not entirely realistic, it sparked my desire to pursue cybersecurity as a career, pushing me to immerse myself in the craft.

Since then, I've worked on various projects and have continuously expanded my skills in both blue-team and red-team scripting.

When I'm not working, you can find me playing video games with my friends, snowboarding, or building penetration testing tools.
